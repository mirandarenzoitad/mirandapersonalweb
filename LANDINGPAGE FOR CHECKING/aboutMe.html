COLLEGE

     In my college days in 1st year, 1st semester, the schedule is great because I have plenty of time to study well and to do the chores and also to take care of myself by going to the gym to maintain my body. I had no problems in 1st semester because the professors are great at teaching and I’m able to sleep well because of not having too many assignments. In 1st year, 2nd semester, things are a little bit draining. The schedule is going past noon, there are many things to note and to study. I won’t be able to do the household chores because I’m investing my time to learn. I also stopped going to the gym. Online classes hurt my eyes a lot because I’m staring at the screen for long periods of time even when I’m taking my notes because it is much more efficient than pen and paper. I don’t even have the time to play video games because after having a day of school sessions I eat then I study and after that, I sleep. Sometimes, when I am loaded with assignments, usually in final periods where almost all subjects have many activities, I can’t even sleep without being sure that I got all the assignments done and reviewed for the quiz. Sleep is very rewarding for me. College life taught me to be more responsible for my schoolwork and proper study habits to make myself productive and efficient. Organization of files, folders, binders, notebooks is the key for me to be prepared. These things are hard to apply, I tried it in 2nd Year, 1st semester. It will make you efficient but I’m neglecting too much of my health that’s why I’m learning to adjust in every schedule that is given. I already know that if I chose to be an irregular student, I will be able to breathe and control more of my time. However, doing these things by the time I will have the thesis with my groupmates, I know that we can’t work out of it due to conflicting schedules. The time that you’ll be only available is the time session of the thesis subject which is not enough. College life for me is hard when it comes to time management, but it is okay to spend a hell of a time for 4 years rather than to spend a lifetime suffering from having low income because of not having enough knowledge. Most importantly, I should enjoy the journey of my college life. Learning to have fun to make memories that I will cherish forever.
