AGRA

     Agra is a city in northern India, located in the state of Uttar Pradesh. It is best known for the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the most famous landmarks in the world. The Taj Mahal was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in childbirth in 1631. It is a beautiful white marble mausoleum that is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, featuring intricate carvings, inlaid gems, and exquisite calligraphy. In addition to the Taj Mahal, Agra is also home to several other historic landmarks, including the Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 16th century. The fort is a massive complex of palaces, mosques, and courtyards, and offers stunning views of the Taj Mahal from its ramparts. Other notable attractions in Agra include the tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah, also known as the Baby Taj, and the Mehtab Bagh, a beautiful garden that offers breathtaking views of the Taj Mahal from across the Yamuna River. Agra is also known for its vibrant culture and cuisine, with a wide range of delicious dishes and street food stalls. Popular dishes include biryani, kebabs, and chaat, a savory snack made with crispy fried dough and spicy toppings.

     Hokkaido is the northernmost and second-largest island of Japan, known for its stunning natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and outdoor activities. It is home to several national parks, ski resorts, hot springs, and scenic landscapes, making it a popular destination for both Japanese and international tourists. One of Hokkaido's most famous attractions is the Sapporo Snow Festival, which is held annually in February and features massive snow sculptures and ice sculptures. The city of Sapporo is also famous for its beer, which is brewed locally and enjoyed throughout Japan. In the summer, visitors can explore Hokkaido's natural beauty by hiking, camping, and fishing in its national parks, including Shiretoko National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The island is also known for its agricultural products, including fresh seafood, dairy products, and fruits like melons and strawberries. During the winter, Hokkaido is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding, with several world-class resorts like Niseko and Rusutsu. STATUE OF UNITY

     The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue of Indian statesman and independence activist Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, located in the western state of Gujarat, India. It is the world's tallest statue, standing at a height of 182 meters (597 feet) from its base to the top of the statue. The statue was inaugurated on October 31, 2018, and is located on a small island in the Narmada River, facing the Sardar Sarovar Dam. The statue was built as a tribute to Patel, who is known as the "Iron Man of India" for his role in unifying the country after independence from British rule in 1947. The Statue of Unity is made of bronze and is supported by a massive steel framework. The statue itself is designed to resemble Patel, with his characteristic Nehru jacket and a walking stick. The base of the statue houses a museum and exhibition hall that showcases Patel's life and achievements. Visitors can take an elevator to a viewing gallery located at a height of 153 meters (502 feet), which offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The site also includes a garden and a shopping and food court area. The construction of the Statue of Unity was a massive undertaking, involving thousands of workers and engineers, and costing over $400 million. It has become a major tourist attraction in India, drawing millions of visitors from around the world each year.
